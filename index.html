Emil Perdue


   Emil is a rising freshman at the University of Chicago where he plans to study both applied mathematics and data science. He serves as the President and Founder of the Greenwich Library Advisory Club, an organization committed to public service through the creation of STEAM events for children and IT programs at several senior citizen homes. He has also founded a low-cost IT company that has supported over 150 senior citizens with their technological deficits. He has also received the Fleishman Service award, the highest distinction for community service in his school district.

   Emil has interned for Congressman Jim Himes for over two years and has worked and spoken directly with presidential candidates. He also serves as the President of the Young Democrats Club where he has organized voter drives, dozens of volunteers for local campaigns, and walk-outs with over 400 students.

   Emil has extensive experience tutoring a variety of subjects, having worked at Zaniac Learning where he has taught various levels of computer programming, mathematics, and robotics to children with both learning abilities and disabilities. During his tenure as Co-President of the Programming Club, he has helped teach and design computer science classes to over 35 students at the high school. He also has over five years of tutoring experience, working primarily in mathematics and computer science. He is a recipient of the AP National Scholar award (17 AP’s total) and has received a 1580 on his SAT and 790 on his Math SAT II.

Subject Areas: Algebra, Geometry, Calculus, Statistics, Physics, Government, Comparative Politics, U.S. History, Computer Science, SAT

Toby Hirsch


   Toby is a senior at Greenwich High School interested in computer science, math and business. He is exceptionally strong in the STEM fields, scoring 800s on the Math II, Physics and Chemistry SAT Subject Tests, along with a perfect 1600 on the SAT. He is currently one of five students at GHS self-studying Linear Algebra and Differential Equations, and he is one of eight students with a perfect score on the Next Generation Science Standards test. He was awarded the Perkins Silver Catalyst Award, a STEM award given to one GHS junior. Toby’s communication skills are also excellent. He is the president of the Greenwich High School Debate Team, where he has been recognized among the top debaters in the state. He was also awarded $1,000 as the First Place Winner of the Connecticut Foundation for Open Government’s Essay Contest, and he was elected governor, the highest office, at Boys State, a leadership program featuring 200 students from across the state.

   Toby’s primary area of interest, however, is computer science. He has created several web applications, including a multiplayer online game and a platform for clubs at Greenwich High School, along with designing and programming many static websites. He is proficient in many programming disciplines, including full-stack web development, mobile application development and machine learning. He has 40+ hours of experience as a private tutor, and he worked at Zaniac, where he taught elementary and middle school students STEM fields.

Subject areas: Math, Science, Computer Science, Humanities, Music, College Essays, Standardized Tests
